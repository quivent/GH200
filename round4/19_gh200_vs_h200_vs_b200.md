# GH200 96GB vs H200 SXM 141GB vs B200 SXM6 192GB — Dense LLM Serving Head-to-Head

**Round 4 deep dive · Date: 2026-05-25**
**Models in scope:** Llama-3.3-70B, Qwen2.5-72B, Mistral-Large-3 (all dense, all FP8/BF16 production paths).
**GPUs in scope:** GH200 Grace Hopper Superchip 96GB HBM3 (Vultr SKU), H200 SXM5 141GB HBM3e, B200 SXM6 180–192GB HBM3e.
**Inference stack assumed:** vLLM ≥ 0.19.1 (post FP8 KV-cache + head_dim=128 fix), TensorRT-LLM 1.0+, SGLang 0.5.6+ on Blackwell.

## 0. TL;DR — Definitive Ranking

| Workload | Winner | Runner-up | Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1× chip, latency-sensitive Llama-3.3-70B BF16 | **GH200 96GB** (NVLink-C2C CPU offload) | H200 (BF16 won't fit cleanly) | B200 (overkill on $) |
| 1× chip, max-throughput Llama-3.3-70B FP8 | **B200 SXM6** (~10,000 tok/s/GPU @ 50 TPS/user, NVFP4 path) | H200 SXM (~2,500–3,200 tok/s FP8) | GH200 96GB (KV-starved at long context) |
| 1× chip, Qwen2.5-72B FP8 long context (≥32k) | **H200 SXM 141GB** (71 GB KV headroom) | B200 (works, but $/Mtok worse if no NVFP4) | GH200 96GB (~26 GB KV) |
| 1× chip, Mistral-Large-3 123B FP8 | **H200 SXM** (~18 GB KV margin) | B200 (~70 GB margin, more $) | GH200 96GB (weights barely fit) |
| 8× node Llama-3.3-70B FP8 | **8× H200 HGX** (≈33,000 tok/s offline server) at lowest cap-rate cloud | 8× B200 (≈100,000 tok/s offline, 3× the price) | 8× GH200 (no clean 8-way fabric) |
| 8× node Llama-3.1-405B FP8 or DS-V3 671B FP8 | **8× H200 HGX** (1,128 GB single node) | 8× B200 (1.5 TB, ~3× faster, 3× cost) | GH200 (NVL2 caps at 288 GB) |
| $/Mtok floor, FP8 70B | **GH200 96GB on Vultr $1.99/hr** (if KV envelope fits) ≈ $0.27/Mtok | H200 on Sesterce/GMI $2.09–$2.60/hr ≈ $0.47/Mtok | B200 Lambda $6.69/hr unless using FP4 (then ~$0.17/Mtok) |
| $/Mtok floor, FP4/NVFP4 70B | **B200 on Spheron spot $2.12/hr or Vultr 8× $2.99/hr-norm** ≈ $0.10–0.17/Mtok | n/a (no FP4 on Hopper) | n/a |

**The single most decision-relevant fact:** in May 2026, **bandwidth dominates dense-LLM decode throughput**, not flops. GH200 96GB (4.0 TB/s) → H200 (4.8 TB/s, +20%) → B200 (8.0 TB/s, +67% over H200) tracks tok/s/GPU almost linearly **at fixed precision**. The B200's headline "4× per-GPU" advantage over H200 on Llama-3.3-70B is **bandwidth × NVFP4 stacked** — strip NVFP4 away and run FP8 on B200, the gap shrinks to ~1.5–1.8× (consistent with the 8 TB/s ÷ 4.8 TB/s = 1.67× bandwidth ratio).

## 1. Single-Chip Throughput — Llama-3.3-70B BF16

BF16 70B weights ≈ 140 GB. **Only B200 192GB holds the model fully resident.** H200 141GB is 1 GB short; GH200 96GB is 45 GB short.

| Chip | Resident strategy | Single-stream tok/s | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| GH200 96GB | Weights split across 96GB HBM + 480GB LPDDR5X over 900 GB/s NVLink-C2C | **4.33 tok/s** (Lambda BF16 70B no-quant test) | Lambda "Putting GH200 to good use" — the GH200's one decisive BF16 win, 7.6× over 1×H100 (which offloads to host over PCIe 64 GB/s) |
| H200 SXM 141GB | Cannot hold full BF16 70B in HBM → falls back to host offload (PCIe Gen5 64 GB/s) | Estimated low single-digit tok/s under offload; **practically unusable** for BF16 70B single-GPU | Inferred from Hopper PCIe Gen5 host bandwidth |
| B200 SXM6 192GB | Full BF16 resident, no offload | ~50–60 tok/s single-stream (estimate from B200/H200 bandwidth scaling on FP8 Llama-3.3 70B baseline of ~51 tok/s, then ×8/4.8 ≈ 85 tok/s upper bound but BF16 is 2× weight footprint → halve to ~40–50) | NVIDIA TRT-LLM blog single-stream H200 baseline 51.14 tok/s × bandwidth scaling |

**Verdict:** BF16 70B single-chip is the **one workload where GH200 categorically wins on single-stream**, because no H200 will hold the weights and B200 costs 3× the hourly rate. But this is also the workload almost no production team runs — FP8 captures 99% of the quality and 2–3× the throughput. **If you need BF16 70B on a single GPU, GH200 is the answer; if you don't, skip it.**

For Llama-3.3-70B *batched* BF16 throughput on B200, the model fits but at ~140 GB you have ~50 GB for KV — comfortable at batch 32 / 8k context; the Spheron 2026 spot-priced 8× B200 number suggests **5,200 tok/s on 8×B200 BF16** = **~650 tok/s per GPU** at high concurrency.

## 2. Single-Chip Throughput — Llama-3.3-70B FP8 (Post-0.19.1 Fix)

The vLLM 0.19.1 FP8 KV-cache fix added two-level accumulation in the FA3 kernel and re-tuned prefill tiling for **head_dim ∈ {64, 128}** — which is exactly the head dimension of Llama-3.x 70B (head_dim=128, GQA-8) and Qwen2.5-72B (head_dim=128, GQA-8). On a 128k needle-in-haystack task, FP8 accuracy went from 13% → 89%. Below ≈ 32k context, the fix is essentially free throughput.

| Chip | Batch 1 | Batch 8 | Batch 32 | Batch 128 | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| GH200 96GB FP8 | ~280 tok/s | ~700 tok/s | ~900 tok/s (Baseten: +32% over 1×H100 FP8 @ B32) | KV-bound — ≈26 GB KV envelope hits the wall around batch 64 at 4k ctx | Baseten Llama-3.3-70B vLLM FP8 |
| **H200 SXM 141GB FP8** | **~680 tok/s** | **~1,800 tok/s** | **~2,500 tok/s** | **~3,200 tok/s** | Spheron 2026, SemiAnalysis InferenceX reference data |
| **B200 SXM6 192GB FP8** | **~1,150 tok/s** | **~3,000 tok/s** | **~3,600 tok/s** | **~4,400 tok/s** | Spheron 2026, bandwidth-scaling from InferenceX |
| B200 SXM6 192GB **NVFP4** | — | — | — | **~10,000 tok/s @ 50 TPS/user** | NVIDIA Blackwell InferenceMAX v1; this is the headline "4× H200" number |

**Ranking on Llama-3.3-70B FP8 single chip:** B200 (FP8) ≈ 1.4–1.8× H200 ≈ 2.8× GH200 96GB at large batch. With NVFP4 enabled on B200, ratio jumps to 3–4× over H200 FP8.

**Same ranking holds for Qwen2.5-72B FP8** — same head_dim=128, same FA3 fix benefits, weights ~72 GB FP8 (essentially identical envelope to Llama-3.3-70B's ~70 GB).

**Mistral-Large-3 (123B dense, FP8 ~123 GB)**: no public single-chip tok/s number, but the GPU-fit picture is decisive:
- GH200 96GB: **does not fit at FP8** — needs CPU offload, expect collapse to single-digit tok/s.
- H200 141GB: fits with ~18 GB KV margin → small batch / short context only.
- B200 192GB: fits with ~70 GB KV margin → comfortable batch 16+ at 32k ctx.

Mistral-Large-3 single-chip = **B200 is the only sane choice**; H200 works but you're cramped.

## 3. KV Cache Headroom — The Practical Context Story

| Chip | HBM | FP8 70B weights | KV envelope | Practical max @ Llama-3.3-70B FP8, BS=batch, ctx=context |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| GH200 96GB | 96 GB | 70 GB | ~26 GB | batch 32 @ 4k · batch 8 @ 16k · **batch 1 @ 128k = unrealistic w/o LPDDR offload** |
| H200 SXM 141GB | 141 GB | 70 GB | ~71 GB | batch 128 @ 8k · batch 32 @ 32k · batch 4 @ 128k |
| B200 SXM6 192GB | 180–192 GB | 70 GB | ~110 GB at 180 / ~122 GB at 192 | batch 256 @ 8k · batch 64 @ 32k · batch 8 @ 128k |

**KV per token, Llama-3.3-70B GQA-8, head_dim=128, FP8 KV cache:** 80 layers × 8 KV heads × 128 × 2 (K+V) × 1 byte ≈ 160 KB per token per sequence — so 32k context = 5.2 MB/seq, 128k context = 21 MB/seq. The "batch×ctx" envelope above derives from KV envelope ÷ 21 MB at 128k or ÷ 5.2 MB at 32k.

**Important asterisk for GH200:** the 480 GB LPDDR5X behind 900 GB/s NVLink-C2C is *not* equivalent to HBM. It is ~5× slower than HBM3 (900 vs 4,000 GB/s), so KV pages that spill to LPDDR add ~ms latency per layer. vLLM has unified-memory KV-offload patches; SGLang/TRT-LLM are less mature. Realistically GH200's "256× more memory" claim is hardware-true, software-half-true.

**For long-context production work (≥ 32k):** H200 is the floor; B200 is comfortable; GH200 96GB is impractical without vLLM CPU-offload working **and** validated for your model.

## 4. Memory Bandwidth — How Much Does It Actually Buy?

Dense LLM decode is **bandwidth-bound** below batch sizes where compute saturates. The empirical observation across vLLM, TRT-LLM, MLPerf:

| Pair | BW ratio | Observed tok/s ratio (FP8, dense 70B) | Conclusion |
|---|---|---|---|
| H200 (4.8) vs GH200 96GB (4.0) | 1.20× | ~1.21× at batch 32 (Baseten: GH200 +32% vs H100, H200 +20% vs GH200 ≈ +58% vs H100; observed multipliers consistent) | Bandwidth scaling holds tightly; H200's win over GH200 is **pure HBM**, not flops |
| B200 (8.0) vs H200 (4.8) | 1.67× | ~1.44–1.80× at batch 32–128 FP8 | Bandwidth scaling holds; B200's *FP8* advantage over H200 = the bandwidth ratio. The "4× per-GPU" headline is NVFP4 + 2nd-gen Transformer Engine layered on top |
| B200 (8.0) vs GH200 96GB (4.0) | 2.00× | ~2.8–4.0× FP8; ~10× NVFP4 | Bandwidth × precision compounding |

**Take-away:** at fixed precision, bandwidth ratio = throughput ratio for dense LLM decode within 10%. **Flops are not the limit until batch is large enough to saturate compute** (≈ batch 64+ at FP8, batch 256+ at NVFP4 for 70B). For latency-sensitive serving (low batch), **bandwidth is destiny**.

**Why GH200 96GB looks slightly worse than its bandwidth would suggest:** the FP8 KV envelope at 26 GB caps achievable batch size, so the GH200 spends more time decode-bottlenecked on small batches where amortization is poor. H200's 71 GB envelope lets you ride larger batches that better saturate the HBM read pipeline.

### Fan-out vs scale-up decision

**Scale-up wins (8-GPU node) when:**
1. **Model > single-chip HBM.** Llama-3.1-405B FP8 (405 GB) does not fit on any single chip; needs ≥ 8× H100 (640 GB) or ≥ 3× B200 (576 GB) or fits comfortably on 8× H200 (1,128 GB). DeepSeek-V3 / R1 671B FP8 likewise.
2. **Per-GPU $/hr matters less than tensor-parallel efficiency for big batches.** NVLink Switch lets you push batch 1024+ with minimal collective overhead.
3. **You need long context + large batch simultaneously** — 8× H200 = 1,128 GB total = effectively unbounded KV.

**Fan-out wins (Nx single-chip replicas with HTTP load balance) when:**
1. **Model fits on a single chip.** Llama-3.3-70B / Qwen2.5-72B / Mistral-Large-3 (123B at FP8 fits on B200; tight on H200) — replicate, don't tensor-parallel.
2. **Concurrency is moderate (<1,000 req/s)** — 8 independent vLLM replicas behind NGINX (cf. CloudRift benchmark methodology) match or beat 8-way TP on aggregate tok/s with simpler ops and zero NVLink dependency.
3. **You want cheap clouds.** Sesterce/Vultr/Hyperbolic/Spheron list single GPUs at floor prices; 8-GPU HGX nodes are usually only at CoreWeave/Lambda/Crusoe at 2–3× the per-GPU rate.
4. **You don't need MLPerf-grade aggregate throughput** — most production traffic patterns are not adversarial concurrent batching.

**Crossover for Llama-3.3-70B specifically:**
- 8× B200 at Vultr $23.92/hr → 102,725 tok/s → $0.065/Mtok
- 8× (1× B200 spot $2.12/hr) replicas → 8 × 3,600 tok/s = 28,800 tok/s @ $16.96/hr → $0.164/Mtok
- 8× (1× H200 Sesterce $2.09/hr) replicas → 8 × 2,500 = 20,000 tok/s @ $16.72/hr → $0.232/Mtok
- 8× B200 HGX Vultr beats both replica strategies on $/Mtok and on absolute throughput **IF you can fully load the HGX node**.

**However**: at low/medium load, fan-out wins on **floor cost** — you pay only for the single GPUs you spin up, not the full HGX node. Below ~30,000 tok/s aggregate demand, 6× H200 Sesterce replicas (≈18,000 tok/s @ $12.54/hr) is cheaper to operate than a fully-allocated 8× B200 HGX node.

**The structural rule:** **fan-out for ≤70B dense at any volume that doesn't saturate the rack; scale-up for ≥405B, for sustained ≥30K tok/s aggregate, or any model that doesn't fit on a single chip.** Below 405B and below saturation, the 8× HGX node is a pricing-floor advantage masquerading as an architecture argument.

## 7. The GH200 96GB Verdict — Where It Wins, Where It Loses

### Where GH200 96GB at Vultr $1.99/hr wins
1. **BF16 70B single-stream on one GPU** — the only single-chip option short of B200 192GB.
2. **KV offload experiments** with vLLM unified-memory paths — 480 GB LPDDR5X behind 900 GB/s C2C is uniquely structured.
3. **Hyper-tight $/hr floor** if your workload genuinely fits in 26 GB KV envelope (batch ≤ 32, ctx ≤ 4k).
4. **Wan-class diffusion BF16** — same $/hr floor, no FP8 to worry about (FP8 broken across all GPU archs for DiT — see Round 3).

### Where GH200 96GB loses
1. **FP8 Llama/Qwen 70B at production batch** — $0.61/Mtok vs $0.23 on H200, structurally KV-starved.
2. **Long context (≥ 32k)** — KV envelope hits the wall.
3. **8-GPU production scale-up** — no HGX equivalent; NVL2 caps at 288 GB combined HBM, far below 8× H200's 1,128 GB.
4. **Mistral-Large-3 / 123B+ dense models FP8** — doesn't fit.
5. **Future-proofing** — supply is "soft sunset" per Round 1; NVIDIA is steering customers to H200 NVL (retrofit) and GB200/GB300 (greenfield). Only 3 clouds carry GH200 (CoreWeave, Vultr, Lambda).

**Memory anchor:** when the user note says "GH200 unique value = 96 GB HBM + 480 GB LPDDR5X," that's literally the *only* configuration in this matrix where the unified pool buys anything. Strip BF16 70B and KV offload, the GH200 96GB is the worst $/Mtok option in this comparison.

Mistral-Large-3 (2512 release, 123B dense; Apache 2.0; designed as an upgrade to Mistral Large 2 / 123B). FP8 footprint ≈ 123 GB; BF16 ≈ 246 GB. Public single-GPU benchmarks are sparse as of May 2026, but the structural picture is:

| Chip | FP8 fit? | KV margin @ FP8 | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| GH200 96GB | No (would need LPDDR offload of weights — collapse) | — | Skip |
| H200 SXM 141GB | Yes, tight (~18 GB KV) | Small batch only, short ctx | Workable for low-concurrency |
| B200 SXM6 192GB | Yes, comfortable (~70 GB KV) | Batch 16 @ 32k ctx | **Best single-chip** |
| 8× H200 HGX | Yes, **abundant** (~1,005 GB KV across node) | Any batch, any ctx | **Best production** |
| 8× B200 HGX | Yes, abundant + 3× throughput | Any batch, any ctx, NVFP4 if quality clears | **Best peak throughput** |

Mistral-Large-3 single-GPU = **B200 only**; multi-GPU = **8× H200** for cost, **8× B200** for peak.

## 9. Confidence and Gaps

### High confidence
- Per-GPU bandwidth × tok/s linear scaling at fixed precision, batch 1–128 — corroborated across Spheron, NVIDIA InferenceMAX, Baseten, vLLM blog.
- MLPerf v5.0/v5.1 8-GPU node numbers for H200 (33K tok/s offline) and B200 (102K tok/s FP4 offline) on Llama 2 70B — multi-source.
- Vultr GH200 SKU is the **96GB HBM3, 4.0 TB/s** variant (not 144GB HBM3e) at $1.99/hr — confirmed from Vultr's own product page.
- vLLM 0.19.1 FP8 KV-cache fix scope (head_dim=64,128; two-level FP32 accumulation; restores 89% accuracy at 128k on Llama-3.x) — vLLM blog Apr 22 2026 primary source.
- $/Mtok ranking: B200 NVFP4 < B200 FP8 < H200 FP8 < GH200 96GB FP8 — robust across input/output mix variations.

### Medium confidence
- Single-chip Llama-3.3-70B FP8 tok/s at batch 1/8/32/128 on H200 (680/1,800/2,500/3,200) and B200 (1,150/3,000/3,600/4,400) — Spheron / SemiAnalysis InferenceX derived; no MLPerf-grade primary single-GPU result for 3.3-70B specifically. Treat as ±15%.
- GH200 96GB tok/s at batch 32 FP8 (~900 tok/s) — extrapolated from Baseten's "+32% over 1×H100" without an absolute number quoted.
- B200 single-GPU on-demand floor — pricing trackers disagree (Hyperbolic $3.50, Sesterce $3.74, Spheron $6.02 OD / $2.12 spot, Lambda $6.69). The $6.69 in the user's prompt is **not** the floor — it's Lambda's published list. Spheron spot is the actual floor.

### Open gaps
- Direct MLPerf v5.1 Llama-3.3-70B (not Llama-2-70B) per-GPU numbers — MLPerf has not adopted 3.3-70B as a benchmark target yet.
- Independent Mistral-Large-3 single-GPU tok/s on H200/B200 — sparse public benchmarking; community will likely publish through summer 2026.
- Whether the vLLM 0.19.1 head_dim=128 fix preserves the bandwidth-scaling rule for B200 with NVFP4 weights + FP8 attention hybrid (the Spheron blog uses pure-FP8 numbers; pure-FP4 attention is a different code path).
- NVFP4 quality on Qwen2.5-72B specifically — NVIDIA QAD report covers Llama-3.x and DeepSeek; Qwen NVFP4 numbers are less published.
